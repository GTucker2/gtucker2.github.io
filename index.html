Howdy! I'm Griffin.

I'm a graduate of the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign with a B.S. in Computer Engineering. I'm a semi-seasoned frontend developer with a special passion for illustration and design.

Professional history.

For four years, I worked with Robb Lindgren and Sammy Lindgren, project leaders with the University of Illinois College of Education and the Office for Mathematics, Science, and Technology Education respectively, as a developer, illustrator, and UI/UX designer to develop educational simulations and demos for educating students of all ages in STEM topics. Our largest and most ambitious project together, an educational simulation of the midwestern water treatment process had me touring the State of Illinois and observing water treatment facilities for the collection of data and visual notes that I later used in the design and implementation of the project's user interface. Read more at Project Clarify.

Special interests.

My history as a UI/UX designer ties into my love for illustration and graphic design; watercolor is especially a good time. I've also always aspired to design my own games, taking inspiration from the amazing independent projects of the past ten years. Owlboy, Fez, Night in the Woods, and Rain World are all killer. Tieing into that, check out Scalapede, my Centipede clone in Scala! Right now I'm really into game design through functional programming. Some other technical interests include: data visualization, AI and machine learning, and NLP.

I took my minor in Scandinavian Studies, so naturally I'll link my favorite content from the north. If you need a good read, Doctor Glas (Hjalmar Söderberg) is great for an evening. Some other goodies include: Out Stealing Horses (Per Petterson), the Ice Palace (Terjei Vesaas), and anything of Tove Jansson's. My favorite folktale is East of the Sun and West of the Moon; Östan om solen och västan om månen. And for the brave souls with pleanty of time on their hands, Fanny and Alexander (Ingmar Bergman) is a great SIX HOUR film. Anyone who can make it through the experience gets a wink and finger-guns from me.
